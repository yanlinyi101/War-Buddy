# v0.15.1 — Terrain DNA (High Ground, Ramps, Naturals, Central Plateau)

Date: 2026-05-13
Project: War Buddy (Godot 4.6.x)
Status: Plan. Awaits v0.15.0 land first.

Parent specs: 06 §4 (spatial vocabulary), 09 §4.6 (elevation rules), 11 §2.4 (nav-obstacles).
Predecessor: v0.15.0 (flat 60×60 base setup).
Followup: v0.15.2 (worker gather loop).

## 1. Goal

v0.15.0 lands a flat 60×60 map with the right *quantities* (1 HQ × 2 sides, 8 mineral + 1 gas × 2 sides) but the wrong *geometry* — no elevation, no chokes, no contested center. The graybox passes data checks but fails to evoke any RTS-tactical reading on screen.

v0.15.1 fixes the geometry. After v0.15.1 the map carries the **three Lost-Temple-soul features**:

1. **High-ground main bases** with single ramp choke (the "defensible cliff")
2. **Natural expansion just outside the ramp** (the "first contest point")
3. **Central elevated plateau** with the gold mine on top (the "control of map = control of vision + economy")

Combined with v0.15.0's mineral/gas placement, the result is a graybox that *reads* as a Lost-Temple-lineage map even at greybox fidelity.

## 2. Lost Temple Feature Gap Audit

From the user's analysis, the three load-bearing geometric DNA elements:

| Feature | LT does | v0.15.0 lacks | v0.15.1 lands |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Elevation** | High / low ground tiles, sight-blocking cliffs | Completely flat | 3 elevation tiers: low (0 m) / mid (1.5 m) / high (3 m) |
| **Main base ramp** | Single ramp choke into main | 4-way open | Single 6 m-wide ramp per main, facing inward |
| **Natural near ramp** | Nat outside ramp, drives early skirmish | No nat concept | 6-mineral nat per main, 4–6 m outside the ramp |
| **Central control** | Center XelNaga plateau / temple | Empty plain | 8×8 m gold-mine plateau, 1.5 m elevation, 3 access ramps |
| **Multi-path** | North + south paths around natural barriers | Open everywhere | 3 paths: north loop / south loop / center-over-plateau |
| **Cliffs / barriers** | Mountains, rivers | None | Cliff walls (NavObstacle + visual) framing the high ground |
| **Map size** | 128×128 SC tiles | 60×60 m | **No size change** — geometry inside 60×60 |
| **Map slots** | 4 (1v1 or 2v2 on any diagonal) | 2 (SW + NE) | v1 keeps 2 slots; 4-slot variant is 09+1 |

Player count stays 2 (1v1) per 09 §2.1 mirror v1; LT's 4-slot symmetry is recorded for 09+1 (per faction expansion to 猫狗/鹅鸭/野生).

## 3. Geometry Design

### 3.1 Three elevation tiers

| Tier | Y | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Low** | 0.0 m | Most of the open map; paths between bases |
| **Mid** | 1.5 m | Central plateau (gold mine seat); minor cliffs framing paths |
| **High** | 3.0 m | Main base platforms (player SW, enemy NE) |

Vertical step heights matter: 1.5 m per tier is "scout climbable via ramp, blocked otherwise" — enforced by NavMesh edges. Godot 4's `NavigationRegion3D` rebakes the same mesh; ramps appear as walkable surfaces stitching low ↔ high.

### 3.2 Main-base high ground

Each main is a **16×16 m platform** at Y=3.0. Contents:

- HQ at platform center
- 8 mineral patches in an arc on the platform (slightly tighter than v0.15.0's arc to fit)
- 1 gas geyser on the platform NW corner

The platform is **bordered by cliffs** (vertical drop to Y=0) on 3 sides, with one **6 m-wide ramp** on the fourth side facing inward (toward enemy).

### 3.2.1 Player main (SW)

```
Y=3.0 platform, footprint approximately:
   x ∈ [-30, -14], z ∈ [14, 30]
   
   (cliff)  (cliff)         (cliff)
    +----------------------------+
    |                            |
    |   m0 m1 m2 m3 m4 m5 m6 m7  |     mineral arc 
    |                            |
    |   g0          HQ          |     HQ at (-22, 3, 22)
    |                            |     gas at (-26, 3, 26)
    |                            |
    +-----------\  /-------------+
                 \ \ <- ramp     
                  \ \ width 6m
                   \ \           
                    \ \          
                     \ \                                     
                      \ V        ramp lands at (-18, 0, 12)
                                 angles ~25° down to low ground
```

Hero respawn at the **bottom of the ramp**, not on the platform — keeps the "commander on the field" feel even at game start.

### 3.2.2 Enemy main (NE)

Mirror about origin. Ramp lands at `(18, 0, -12)`.

### 3.3 Natural expansion (the "nat")

Each main gets a **6-mineral nat** at low elevation, **4 m outside the ramp landing**:

Player nat:
- Anchor: `(-14, 0, 8)` (4 m east of ramp bottom, opening south-east toward center)
- 6 mineral patches in a tight arc (no gas geyser at the nat in v1; gas-second-nat is 09+1)

Enemy nat mirror at `(14, 0, -8)`.

The nat exists at low ground — **no cliffs around it**. It is *defensible by holding the ramp above* (sight + ranged-fire from the high ground covers the nat) but cannot itself be fortified by terrain. This is the Lost-Temple-soul "you control your nat by controlling your ramp" relationship.

### 3.4 Central plateau (the "temple")

A mid-elevation (Y=1.5) **8×8 m platform** centered at origin `(0, 1.5, 0)`. Contains:

- **1 gold mine** — single mineral patch with `initial_amount = 3000` and `harvest_amount_per_cycle = 8` (vs regular 1500 / 5). Cyan tint shifted to **yellow-gold** for visual distinction.
- **No gas geyser** on the plateau in v1

Access: **3 ramps**, each 5 m wide, facing N / W / S (no E ramp — that would be 4-way but we want some asymmetric vision tension). Walking up any ramp reveals the plateau and the gold; the gold is hidden by fog from low ground when fog ships (deferred).

Visually: 4 cliff faces on the plateau edges where ramps don't connect, framing it as a distinct landmark.

### 3.5 Multi-path map skeleton

The 60×60 map splits into 3 traversable paths between the two mains:

| Path | Route | Properties |
|---|---|---|
| **North loop** | From player ramp → north along Y=0 → past plateau N ramp → enemy ramp | Longest; passes through plateau's N ramp |
| **South loop** | Mirror via south side | Same length |
| **Center** | Up player ramp → through center plateau → down enemy ramp | Shorter but goes over high ground; vulnerable to vision |

To enforce these paths rather than letting units roam everywhere: cliff walls flank each path with ~10 m corridor width — wide enough for army movement, narrow enough that a unit can't just walk through the middle of the map ignoring the paths.

### 3.6 Cliff walls / nav barriers

Implemented as:

- **Visual**: cuboid `MeshInstance3D` of dark-grey rock material lining the cliff edges
- **Collision**: `StaticBody3D` on the `terrain` physics layer with `NavigationObstacle3D` attached, baked into the nav-mesh as carved-out regions
- **Vision-blocking**: cliffs block line-of-sight queries used by sight_range computations (deferred to fog-of-war pass; 09 §4.6 already mandates the rules)

## 4. Spatial Vocabulary (07 §3 hookup)

The new geometry feeds into 07's spatial schema:

### 4.1 Grid overlay (07 §3.1)

Re-bake the 60×60 map's grid to **12×12** (A1–L12), 5 m per cell. (8×8 was for the v0.15.0 small map; 12×12 fits a "double-sized Lost-Temple-lineage" map better.) `MapGrid.cell_size = Vector2(5, 5)`.

### 4.2 Designer landmarks (07 §3.2)

These ship as `Landmark.tres` files in `res://data/maps/<map_id>/landmarks/`:

| `landmark_id` | Display | Cells (approximate) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| `player_main` | "main" / "home" / "base" | E3–F4 region | Player HQ platform |
| `player_ramp` | "my ramp" / "the choke" | E5 | Player ramp landing |
| `player_nat` | "natural" / "second" / "nat" | F5–G5 | Player natural expansion |
| `enemy_main` | "enemy base" / "their main" / "across" | G9–H10 | Enemy HQ platform |
| `enemy_ramp` | "enemy ramp" / "their choke" | G8 | Enemy ramp landing |
| `enemy_nat` | "enemy nat" / "their nat" | F8 | Enemy natural |
| `central_plateau` | "temple" / "high ground" / "middle" / "center" | F6–G7 | Center plateau |
| `gold_mine` | "gold" / "rich mine" / "center gold" | F6 | Gold patch (subset of plateau) |
| `north_path` | "north" / "top" / "north corridor" | row 3 | North loop |
| `south_path` | "south" / "bottom" / "south corridor" | row 10 | South loop |

10 landmarks total. The deputy LLM can now resolve utterances like "守住坡口", "去中央拿金矿", "走北路骚扰" against meaningful named cells.

### 4.3 Player-defined regions

Per 06 §4.3 / 07 §3.3, players can layer their own region names. With actual terrain, this becomes valuable — a player can name "the ramp I always defend from" as `defender_perch` referencing 1–2 cells on the platform edge. v0.15.1 ships the data path (player_region resource format already locked); the war-room editor (10 §4.5) ships when 10 is built.

### 5.3 Central

| Resource | Position | Amount | Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| `gold_mine` | `(0, 1.5, 0)` | 3000 | MID |

Gold mine harvest rate is `harvest_amount_per_cycle = 8` (vs regular 5) — a 60% premium per cycle. The plateau is **un-defensible by terrain** (3 ramps, anyone can enter), so the premium is paid in vulnerability rather than safety. This is the "contested center" pattern.

## 8. Risk and Rollback

- **Risk:** nav-mesh bake produces disconnected islands at ramp junctions if cell sizes are wrong. Mitigation: §6.1 calls for explicit small `cell_height` during bake; test with hero pathfinding from spawn to HQ before committing.
- **Risk:** v0.15.0 entity Y coordinates collide with the new platform (workers spawn inside the cliff). Mitigation: §6.2 explicitly re-anchors every existing entity; tested by `test_v015_1_world_init.gd`.
- **Risk:** the platform footprint is too small for the 8 mineral patches + HQ + gas + 6 workers. Mitigation: tightened to 16×16 from v0.15.0's looser arrangement; visualize in editor before commit.
- **Risk:** designer landmarks reference grid cells that don't match actual cliff geometry (e.g. `player_ramp = E5` but the ramp is actually at D5). Mitigation: §6.5 + §6.6 are done together; `test_grid_resolution.gd` pins specific world ↔ cell mappings.

Rollback: revert §6 commits in reverse order; map returns to v0.15.0 flat geometry.

## 10. Open Questions

- **Should the gold mine be visible from low ground?** With the plateau at 1.5 m and the gold patch on top, low-ground units may technically see it. Once fog-of-war + sight rules ship (v0.16), the answer depends on `sight_range` of typical scouting units. For v0.15.1: gold mine is always visible (no fog yet). Document the v0.16 transition explicitly.
- **Ramp width**: 6 m for main ramp, 5 m for plateau ramps. Tunable based on early playtest of unit traversal. If frontline_basic + ranged_basic can't both squeeze through 6 m comfortably, widen to 7 m.
- **Cliff art**: graybox = single grey-rock material. When art pass lands, cliffs should have biome-appropriate variants (forest / desert / coast etc.). Not in v0.15.1 scope.
- **Should we also implement the elevation rule (50% miss low-attacking-high) in v0.15.1, or wait for v0.16?** Recommend waiting — fog of war is the natural co-feature, and shipping the miss rule without vision-blocking would feel arbitrary.
